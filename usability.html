Pinterest Usability Study

–– Usability study analysis on Pinterest, completed with classmates Akansha Dey, Brent Bosart, and Armand DiLisio ––

For this project, our team posed the questions:

  1. How do the purposes and goals of Pinterest usage vary, if at all, amongst male and female Generation Z users?
  2. What aspects of Pinterest’s functionalities aid, hinder or are irrelevant to the goals of experienced users?

Target audience and participant demographic:

  • Gen. Z Pinterest users that have experience using the platform, with an even spread of male and female participants.

Methods:

  • Virtual interview through conference call (members: 1 interviewer / moderator, 2 notetakers, 1 observer, participant)
  • UAR (Usability Aspect Report) development using interview notes and footage
  • Analysis and comparison of critical points, severity of problems, and relationship of problems between UARs

Findings:

  • Male and female usage of the Pinterest have no significant differences
  • Pinterest usage is predominantly for personal reasons such as hobbies, interests, and inspiration
  • Most Pinterest users do not engage with the social functions that are available
  • Further, many Pinterest users are unaware of how to use these social functions

Recommendations:

  • Converting the "follow" feature to a "Subscription" feature, allowing users to subscribe to content creators that fit their personal needs
  • Toggle the social aspect of Pinterest, allowing users to turn these features off could reduce the cognitive load on their screen
  • Change nothing at all; the social functionalities do not aid, nor hinder, the user's goal

Creating a Clockface

–– Laser-cut clockface created through the use of sketching and Adobe Illustrator ––

This project was a class assignment, where the prompt was to create a clockface for your room that would demonstrate an understanding using a laser-cutter. I began this process by brainstorming clockfaces that would fit aesthetically in my room. I developed three clockfaces, and chose one.

Next, I iterated over the sketch using Adobe Illustrator and made minor changes. I adjusted the document size, then submitted this file to the MSU MakerSpace laser cutter to create the piece.

Finally, using the clock mechanism provided by our professor, I put the clock together.
